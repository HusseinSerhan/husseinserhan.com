Culture

I grew up in Venezuela. My immigrant parents have different religions. I moved to Canada and met Europeans, Africans, Chinese, Koreans and more. This led me to explore how my culture has influenced my behaviour.

  • Is Anti-Semitism Generic?. "Many of the explanations of anti-Jewish attitudes and actions over the centuries, including mob violence and mass expulsions, have focused on things unique to Jews or unique to the Christian-Jewish relationship in Europe or the Muslim-Jewish relationship in the Middle East. Yet many of the same attitudes and actions—and some of the very same words and phrases—have been directed at other groups which have had none of the factors which are said to explain anti-Jewish attitudes and actions among Christians and Muslims. What these other groups—the Armenians in the Ottoman Empire, Ibos in Nigeria, Marwaris in Burma, overseas Chinese in Southeast Asia, and Lebanese in a number of countries—have had in common with the Jews has not been religion, race, or language, but their economic and social roles."

    • Sowell explores why Jews, Koreans, Chinese, Lebanese and other ethnic minorities succeed. He concludes that placing a higher value on education and rejecting "hood" culture is the key. Contrary to what Latinos and African Americans do.

    Controversial as it is. Sowell seems genuine in his attempt to determine why his community is lagging.

    My dad is Lebanese-Muslim and my mother is Colombian-Christian. Both met in Venezuela as young immigrants without education or strong family ties.

    Sowell helped me understand why my father could not tolerate poor academic performance. And why my mother could.

  • Who Are the Most Successful Immigrants in the World?.

    • Taleb talks about the Lebanese diaspora. It is self-serving. Yet, there are successful Lebanese everywhere. It also ties well with Sowell's article.

  • Our Culture, What's Left of It: The Mandarins and the Masses

    • Dalrymple explores poverty and criminality in the UK. The book gave me an insight into the criminal world surrounding me growing up in Caracas.

I love learning about this subject. If you know another author or researcher working on it, please send it my way. .
